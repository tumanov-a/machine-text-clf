More about the features

For the project we extracted 61 feature based on previous studies and our observations from the dataset.

All features can be divided into 7 groups according to their targeted branch of linguistics or functionality:

  • Descriptive metrics
  • Frequency features
  • Avg features
  • Morphological features:
    • part of speech frequency
    • grammar forms
  • Lexical features which indicate lexical diversity of the text
  • Readability features
  • Cohesion features

Out of all features, only Adverbial participle and Numerals proved to not be significant in Mann-Whitney statistical test; Density of unique words, Frequency (according to Sharovâ€™s dictionary) and Prepositional case (Noun) proved to not be significant in T-test.

Here are some examples of the most prominent features that help distinguish AI generated text from a real one. If you want to read more about the features and results of the statistical texts, check out our paper.

Average number of words in a sentence. Avg feature.

Both T-test and Mann-Whitney U test have found significant difference in the mean values of Average number of words in a sentence for two classes. Mean value for Human class is 20.7 and 17.9 for Machine class.

Verbs. Morphological feature.

As a part of speech, verbs in data are also found to be a significantly important feature. Though classes' means do not differ much, 0.090 for Human and 0.089 for Machine, the difference is still significant, as p-value is less than 0.05 in cases of both statistical tests.

Density of punctuation symbols. Frequency feature.

An example of a significant frequency feature. For Human and Machine class the means are 0.031 and 0.28 respectively.
